Customers Speed Innovation and Success in the Cloud with Leading Technology and Services from Oracle and Wipro Limited

Wipro Limited (NYSE: WIT, BSE: 507685, NSE: WIPRO), a leading global information technology, consulting and business process services company, today announced that it has joined Oracle's newly announced Oracle Cloud Managed Service Provider (MSP) Program. Wipro is a Diamond level member of Oracle PartnerNetwork (OPN) and has an OPN Cloud Select designation.

The Wipro Boundaryless Data Center (BLDC) offering is based on an innovative IT transformational approach that enables enterprise ITaaS (IT as a Service) through a universal marketplace. BLDC is integrated with Oracle IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service) and PaaS (Platform as a Service) services, and provides a unified platform to drive hybrid IT journey for enterprises by enabling dynamic sourcing, automated provisioning, monitoring and management. In collaboration with Oracle, Wipro will build standard reference architectures, workload blueprints and continuous deployment solutions for accelerated cloud transformation.

Kiran Desai, Senior Vice President and Business Head, Global Infrastructure Services (GIS), Wipro Limited said, "We are excited to be a part of the Oracle Managed Services Partner program (MSP), to help enterprises build a scalable hybrid IT model, leveraging Wipro's Boundaryless Data Center (BLDC) platform to accelerate their digital journey. The BLDC platform provides end-to-end life cycle services across Oracle IaaS and PaaS, to help customers enhance the speed of execution and improve business performance."

Available to partners who have demonstrated the skills and infrastructure to build, deploy, run and manage both Oracle and non-Oracle workloads, the Oracle Cloud Managed Service Provider (MSP) Program, enables OPN members to offer a complete managed service solution for workloads running on Oracle Platform as a Service andInfrastructure as a Service.

Based on the unique needs of their customers, Wipro can now have the ability to provide a complete, integrated cloud solution, leveraging its Boundaryless Data Center (BLDC) and Oracle's world-class cloud platform.

"Cloud represents a huge opportunity for our customers to increase innovation and agility, while enabling reduced cost and complexity," said Sanjay Sinha, Vice President, Platform Products, Oracle. "Oracle's leading cloud infrastructure, coupled with the services expertise of Wipro, will provide customers with the foundation, they need to accelerate success in the cloud."

The Oracle Cloud Marketplace offers an intuitive user interface to browse and search for available applications and services, as well as user ratings and reviews to help customers determine the best business solutions for their organization. With its new automated application installation features, customers can easily deploy provider business applications from a centralized cloud interface.

Blockchain, in simple terms, is a universal ledger that maintains records and manages transactions through a reliable publicly accessible database. The technology eliminates the need of a central authority (like a bank) to authenticate a transaction. These transactions are secured by advanced cryptography. The consensus method of transaction approval also makes the system extremely difficult to hack. The assessee also provides information technology and network support services to its Associate Enterprises (AEs).

During AY 2008-09, the assessee provided information technology and software support services to its AE including remote monitoring of information technology system, trouble shooting of information technology related problem, back up related task. To benchmark the transaction, assessee adopted TNMM as the MAM with Operating Profit to Operating Cost (OP/OC) as Profit Level Indicator (PLI). The assessee selected 23 companies as comparables using multiyear data with average margin of 14.41% to demonstrate that its margin of 13.97% was at ALP.

TPO accepted TNMM as MAM with OP/OC as PLI, however, by applying some fresh set of filters, finalized 23 companies as comparables with arithmetic mean of 24.99% and proposed a TP adjustment of Rs 8.45 lakhs. DRP confirmed TPO’s order.

Aggrieved, the assessee preferred an appeal before Mumbai ITAT.

Turnover filter

Before the ITAT, assessee contended that companies having turnover of more than Rs 20 crore should be excluded as the assessee turnover was only Rs 87.39 lakhs.

ITAT noted that the TPO had himself applied turnover filter of less than Rs 1 crore, however, did not fix any upper limit while applying the turnover filter. In this regard, ITAT opined that, “When the Transfer Pricing Officer has applied turnover filter by rejecting companies having less than Rs 1 crore turnover from software development services, logically, he should have fixed an upper limit for rejecting companies having very high turnover as well”. ITAT also noted that against the assessee turnover of Rs 87.39 lakhs from software development services, some of the comparables selected by the assessee had turnover of more than Rs 20 crores viz. Infosys Technologies Ltd., Tata Elxsi Ltd. and Wipro Ltd with Rs 15,653 crores, Rs 342 crores and Rs 11,276 crores respectively.

ITAT observed that DRP, while upholding the turnover filter adopted by the TPO, referred to OECD guidelines to observe that company with approximately five times revenue could be taken as comparable. Applying the same logic of the DRP, ITAT held that, “companies with extraordinarily high turnover cannot be treated as comparable”.

Relying on the High Court decision in Pentair Water India Pvt. Ltd [TS-566-HC-2015(BOM)-TP], ITAT held that, “Companies which are having more than 20 times the turnover of the assessee from software development services segment…cannot be treated as comparables”. Accordingly, ITAT directed the TPO to exclude companies having turnover of more than Rs 20 crores from software development services segment from the list of comparables.

Infosys Technologies Ltd

Specifically with regard to Infosys Technologies Ltd, Revenue submitted that this company was chosen as a comparable by assessee in its TP-study as well as during course of proceedings before TPO. Revenue contended that assessee had at no stage objected the selection of Infosys Technologies Ltd and hence without raising an additional ground in terms of rule 11 of IT (AT) Rule, 1963, the assessee could not raise the issue at this stage.

ITAT noted that assessee had selected Infosys Technologies Ltd. as a comparable in the transfer pricing study and even in the course of proceedings before TPO. Further, ITAT stated that from TPO’s order, it was clearly evident that Infosys Technologies Ltd was proposed as comparable after search in database. Also, ITAT noted that assessee had objected to wrong application of turnover filter by the TPO not only before the Tribunal but before the DRP also. In view of the above, ITAT held that, “For the first time assessee has not raised the issue of rejection of certain comparables on the basis of turnover” and refused to accept the contention of the Revenue.
